How the model works

Each activity is tagged with the brain regions it plausibly engages, based on the directional findings of the studies above (e.g. meditation → improved amygdala/PFC regulation; aerobic exercise → hippocampal BDNF; repeated action → striatal automaticity; passive scrolling → reduced PFC/attention-network engagement). Logging an activity nudges the affected region's score up or down, scaled by duration, skill level, and your current streak (consistency multiplier, capped at the ~66-day automaticity benchmark from Lally et al., 2010). Scores drift back toward neutral (50) if a behavior isn't repeated — reflecting the "use it or lose it" nature of synaptic plasticity. This is a simplified, illustrative model for education and self-reflection, not a clinical or diagnostic measurement of your actual brain.
